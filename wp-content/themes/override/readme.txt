== Description ==
Description: Override theme for wordpress is sophisticated, lightweight, and adaptable skeleton theme. Make it yours with a custom menu, header image, and background -- then go further add your contact form and split your posts into two or three columns via custom templates. Override comes equipped with a widget support galore (left-sidebar, right-sidebar, four footer areas), support for featured images (as large custom header images on posts, pages and "sticky" posts).

== Installation ==

1. In your admin panel, go to Appearance -> Themes and click the 'Add New' button.
2. Type in Override in the search form and press the 'Enter' key on your keyboard.
3. Click on the 'Activate' button to use your new theme right away.
4. Navigate to Appearance > Customize in your admin panel and customize to taste.

== Frequently Asked Questions ==

= How to split my posts into two or three columns ? =

1. Navigate to Pages -> Add New and create new page.
2. From template dropdown ( inside a Page Attributes section ) choose Blog Two Columns or Blog Three Columns template.
3. Click Publish.
4. Navigate to Settings -> Reading -> Front page displays ,then tick "A static page"
5. Make sure that Posts page dropdown is EMPTY/NOT selected ( leave it blank as -Select- )
6. From a Front page dropdown choose your template and click Save changes. 
7. Your posts will be shown on Front Page in two/three columns. 

= How to show Two/Three columns template on other pages ( not on Front page ) ? =

Follow all the steps above from 1 to 4 if you haven't done it already. 
On step 5 choose your Two/Three column page template on Posts page dropdown.
On step 6 From a Front page dropdown choose your Static page and click Save changes ( if you leave this blank as -Select- your Front page will show posts in one column layout ).
Your posts will be shown on other Page ( not on Front Page ) in two/three columns and your choosed Page will be shown as Front Page.


= How to add menu descriptions to my Menu Items ? =

1. Navigate to Appearance -> Menues.
2. Tick/choose Main Menu beside Display location.
3. Add a name to your menu beside Menu Name.
4. Click Save Menu.
5. Click "Screen Options" located on top right corner.
6. Tick "Description" box.
7. On pages ( left side ) tick the boxes beside your pages names to add those pages to your menu.
8. Click Add to Menu.
9. Inside Menu Structure ( right side ) click the arrow on the right side of your menu item to reveal additional configuration options.
10. Enter description into description box and click Save Menu.
